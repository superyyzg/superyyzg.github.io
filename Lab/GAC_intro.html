Introduction to Generalization-Aware Compression of Deep Neural Networks

Overview

Deep neural networks (DNNs) have achieved remarkable success across a wide range of applications. However, their large size and computational cost make deployment on resource-constrained devices challenging. Model compression techniques aim to reduce model size and computation while preserving performance.

What is Generalization-Aware Compression?

Generalization-aware compression focuses not only on reducing model complexity but also on maintaining or improving the model’s ability to generalize to unseen data, including out-of-distribution (OOD) settings. This is especially important in real-world applications where data distributions can shift.

Key Ideas

  • Balance model efficiency and predictive performance
  • Preserve robust and transferable representations
  • Reduce reliance on domain-specific features
  • Enhance robustness to noise and distribution shifts

Conclusion

Generalization-aware compression is a promising direction that combines efficiency with robustness. By integrating theoretical insights with practical design, it enables scalable and reliable deployment of deep learning models in real-world settings.
